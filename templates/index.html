    Greenhouse Gas UK

    Greenhouse gases trap solar heat in the atmosphere, keeping the Earth warm enough to sustain life.

    Human activities such as burning fossil fuels,and land clearing are increasing the amount of greenhouse released into the atmosphere. This is trapping extra heat and causing the Earth's Temprature to rise.

    • The most important Greenhouse gas in UK(and on Earth) by volume and impact on the atmosphere is Carbon dioxide(CO2). All other greenhouse gasses are measured as carbon dioxide equivalent(CO2e).
    • Methane(CH4), Nitrous oxide(N2O), Hydrofluorocarbons(HFC),Perfluorocarbons(PFC) and Sulphur hexaflouride(SF8) are the other significant greenhouse gasses in the UK(and on Earth).

    Our scatter plot shows that there is a negative correlation between the population of the Uk and greenhouse gas emmision in the period reviewed. While the population of the UK grew from 57,237,000 in 1999, to 66,797,000 in 2019, greenhouse gas emission fell from 809.1 to 454.8 (million tonnes CO2e) between 1999 and 2019.

    In 1990 Coal is the singular fuel source of greenhouse gas emissions at 221.1 million tonnes COe, followed by Natural gas at 109.6 million tonnes CO2e.

    By 2006,emissions from Coal(137.7) has reduced significantly,droping to second behind Natural gas(192.1),almost double the 1990 value. Diesel(64.2) overtook Motor spirit(57.4) compared to 1990.Total emissions reduced.

    Emmisions from Natural gas(160.0) reduced in 2019,but it is still the highest source of Greenhouse gas in the UK.Emission from Diesel(76.2) continues to grow, while Coal(11.9) is no longer a major source of Greenhouse gas emissions in the UK. Total emission is almost half of the 1990 value.
